How we met

We met during our college days, starting out with no expectations of what was to come. Music became the bridge that brought us closer, especially when we joined a duet singing contest at school. From practice to the final performance, everything felt easy and natural, like we had quietly found a rhythm together.

It was only after, as we kept talking and staying connected, that something began to grow. Through our conversations, we started to see each other differently, and little by little, something special found its way into our hearts.

What began as a simple duet turned into a love we never expected, yet one that gently grew into something real and lasting. And now, as we prepare to say “I do,” we step forward with quiet certainty, carrying the same love that led us here—into forever.

Gentle reminders

With warmth and gratitude—a few details that help our day feel smooth and welcoming for everyone.

Arriving at the church

We would be so grateful if you could join us at Our Lady of Perpetual Help with a little time to spare before the ceremony—arriving by about 3:45 PM for our 4 PM start usually gives everyone a calm moment to find seats before we begin.

Attire & colors

It would mean a great deal to us if you could dress as your invitation suggests and echo the color palette the bride and groom have chosen. The Dress code button above shares the shades and styling notes—thank you for helping our photos and room feel beautifully cohesive.

Our guest list

We've planned this celebration lovingly around the friends and family. Regrettably, we are not able to welcome children or additional plus-ones, as seating and catering are arranged for our confirmed guest count alone. Your understanding truly makes a difference—thank you.

RSVP & updates

Your reply by the date noted in our RSVP section helps us hold a place for you and share an accurate head count with our catering team—we are so thankful for every timely response. If plans shift after you've RSVP'd, please send us a message so we may coordinate updates with catering together wherever possible.

Should we receive your RSVP after that date, or should someone arrive without an earlier response, we may regretfully need to withhold a seat, as tables and plated meals close with the caterer's final numbers. We're truly sorry whenever that proves necessary, and we're grateful for your gracious understanding.
